Expert Panel Insights

Product Strategist

Product & Retention Lens

Product-Market Fit Retention Differentiation
Strengths
  • Zero financial risk lowers the onboarding barrier to near zero — ideal for first-time investors.
  • Leaderboard creates a lightweight social layer that drives return visits.
  • Real price data (Finnhub) makes practice feel authentic, building genuine skill transfer.
  • Cross-platform (web + iOS + Android) maximises reachable audience.
Weaknesses
  • No "graduation" flow — users who outgrow paper trading have no next step within the app.
  • Minimal educational scaffolding; the app teaches by doing but offers no guided learning.
  • Single portfolio per user limits advanced simulation scenarios (e.g. testing strategies side-by-side).
Risks & Missed Opportunities
  • Without community features (comments, strategy sharing), retention plateaus after novelty wears off.
  • Missed: in-app achievements / badges would extend the habit loop at near-zero cost.
Growth & Marketing Expert

Acquisition & Funnel Lens

Virality Pricing Psychology Channels
Strengths
  • $1 → $10,000 virtual cash top-up is a brilliant micro-commitment that normalises payment before users see real value.
  • Leaderboard is inherently shareable — "I'm #3 worldwide" is social currency.
  • App Store presence in the "investing" niche benefits from consistently high organic search intent.
  • Low CAC opportunity: content marketing (TikTok/YouTube "learn to trade" niche) is highly effective here.
Weaknesses
  • No referral loop — every new user costs money or luck to acquire.
  • The $1 IAP is priced so low it attracts impulsive purchases but caps revenue potential per user.
Risks & Missed Opportunities
  • Apple/Google take 30% on IAP — margins shrink without a web payment fallback.
  • Missed: shareable portfolio snapshots (e.g. "My 30-day return card") would drive organic installs for free.
  • No email capture means no re-engagement channel when users go dormant.
Financial Analyst

Revenue & Unit Economics Lens

LTV Margins Scalability
Strengths
  • Infrastructure cost scales predictably: Vercel/Amplify + PostgreSQL + Finnhub API have clear tiered pricing.
  • Existing Stripe integration removes friction for expanding to subscriptions or larger IAP bundles.
  • No inventory, no fulfilment — pure software margin profile once acquisition cost is amortised.
Weaknesses
  • Current ARPU is extremely low: even a power user who tops up 5× generates only $5 gross revenue.
  • Finnhub API costs rise linearly with MAU and tick frequency — potential margin compression at scale.
  • No recurring revenue stream; all revenue is transactional and unpredictable.
Risks & Missed Opportunities
  • Single-revenue-line dependency: if the $1 IAP loses appeal, revenue collapses.
  • Missed: a subscription model would enable financial forecasting and investor storytelling.
  • No free trial to premium conversion funnel currently in place.

Monetization Models

💎

Model 1 — Freemium + Monthly Subscription

Tiered access with a recurring revenue floor
Revenue Potential: HIGH
Why it fits: FauxFolio already has a clear free experience (basic trading) and natural premium features to gate (advanced analytics, multiple portfolios, custom watchlists, AI-assisted insights). Subscription normalises monthly spend and converts one-time buyers into predictable MRR.
Pricing Structure
Free $0 / mo
1 portfolio · 5 watchlist slots · standard simulator
Pro $4.99 / mo
3 portfolios · unlimited watchlist · advanced P&L charts · early leaderboard access
Trader+ $9.99 / mo
Unlimited portfolios · AI trade suggestions · news feed · CSV export · $50k starting balance
Pros & Cons
✓Predictable MRR — fundable, forecastable growth story
✓Aligns incentives: more features → higher retention
✓Web payment avoids 30% App Store cut
✗Requires significant feature investment to justify $4.99+
✗Churn management is ongoing operational work
✗Free tier must remain compelling to avoid negative reviews
Estimated Revenue Potential

At 10,000 MAU with a 5% conversion to Pro ($4.99): $2,495 MRR (~$30k ARR) in year 1. At 100k MAU with 8% conversion mix: ~$350k ARR. High ceiling — this is the primary growth engine.

🎯

Model 2 — Enhanced Virtual Cash IAP Bundles

Expand on the existing $1 micro-purchase with tiered bundles and tournaments
Revenue Potential: MEDIUM
Why it fits: The $1/$10k IAP is already live and validated. Expanding to bundles, one-time "season pass" tournaments, and time-limited challenges converts engagement spikes into revenue without requiring a subscription relationship.
Pricing Structure
Starter Pack $0.99
+$10,000 virtual cash (current)
Booster Pack $2.99
+$50,000 · bonus 2× multiplier for 7 days
Tournament Entry $1.99 / event
30-day leaderboard competition · exclusive badge · featured placement
Fresh Start $0.99
Reset portfolio + restore starting $10k balance
Pros & Cons
✓Low friction — users already conditioned to $1 purchases
✓Tournaments drive engagement spikes and press coverage
✓No new infrastructure — uses existing Stripe + balance system
✗Revenue is lumpy; no predictable monthly floor
✗IAP subject to 30% platform fee unless web checkout is default
✗Can feel "pay-to-win" if not carefully balanced
Estimated Revenue Potential

If 3% of 10,000 MAU purchase a bundle monthly at avg $1.99: ~$600 MRR. Tournaments could spike to $3k+ per event. Solid complement to subscriptions but cannot stand alone as the primary model.

🎓

Model 3 — Affiliate / Brokerage Referral Program

Monetise the "graduation moment" when users are ready to trade real money
Revenue Potential: MEDIUM–HIGH
Why it fits: FauxFolio's core promise is learning to invest. The natural user journey ends with wanting to trade real money. Partnering with brokerages (Webull, Robinhood, Interactive Brokers, etc.) on a CPA referral model converts FauxFolio's best users — those confident enough to go real — into high-value, near-zero-cost revenue.
Pricing Structure
CPA — Account Open $25–$75 / referral
Standard brokerage affiliate payout per funded account
Revenue Share 0.1–0.3% of trades
Some brokerages offer ongoing rev-share on referred user activity
Sponsored "Go Live" Banner Flat sponsorship fee
Featured placement within app for broker brand visibility
Pros & Cons
✓Zero product development cost — pure distribution play
✓High CPA payouts from brokerages ($25–$200+)
✓Aligns with user intent — users want to trade real money eventually
✗Dependent on brokerage partnerships — takes time to negotiate
✗Conversion rates are low (~1–3% of active users)
✗Must be disclosed (FTC); perceived conflict of interest if aggressive
Estimated Revenue Potential

At 10,000 MAU, 2% conversion to a real brokerage account at $40 CPA avg: ~$8,000 / month passively. Scales directly with user base growth. At 100k MAU this becomes $80k/month — the highest per-unit revenue of any model listed.

Final Recommendation

Panel Consensus · Primary Strategy

Freemium Subscription + Brokerage Referral as Multiplier

The subscription model (Model 1) is the right primary engine: it establishes predictable MRR, aligns feature investment with revenue, and gives investors a clear ARR narrative. The brokerage referral program (Model 3) is not a separate model — it's a multiplier activated when users naturally outgrow paper trading. These two reinforce each other: subscriptions keep users engaged longer, and longer engagement means a higher-quality, more convertible referral audience. IAP bundles (Model 2) are a low-effort revenue complement, not a foundation.
Implementation Roadmap
1
Email capture + engagement baseline (Week 1–2)
Add email field to registration or a post-login prompt. Build a simple 7-day return rate dashboard to understand current retention before changing monetisation.
Foundation
2
Expand IAP bundles & add portfolio reset (Week 2–3)
Ship the Booster Pack ($2.99) and Portfolio Reset ($0.99) IAPs. Validate payment flow end-to-end and confirm Stripe webhook idempotency works for new products. Low effort, immediate ARPU lift.
Quick Win
3
Build Pro feature set (Month 1–2)
Prioritise 3 high-perceived-value features: (1) multi-portfolio support, (2) extended P&L analytics charts, (3) unlimited watchlist. Gate these behind a "Pro" flag in the DB. Do not launch subscription until the value is clearly demonstrable.
Product Work
4
Launch subscription via web-first checkout (Month 2)
Launch Pro at $4.99/month via Stripe on the web app first — avoids the 30% App Store cut during validation. Use a 7-day free trial. Only add IAP subscription SKU to App Store after web conversion rate is confirmed positive.
Revenue Milestone
5
Add "Ready to go real?" referral CTA (Month 3)
Surface a contextual banner for users who have been active 30+ days or have a positive simulated return. Link to a brokerage partner via affiliate URL. Disclose clearly. Target: Webull or Interactive Brokers (both have active affiliate programs, $50–$200 CPA).
Revenue Multiplier
6
Launch monthly trading tournament (Month 3–4)
$1.99 entry, 30-day leaderboard, winner gets a cosmetic badge and featured profile. Drives re-engagement spikes and earned media. Promote on social with shareable "My Tournament Return" cards.
